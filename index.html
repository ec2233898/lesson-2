Edinburgh

Edinburgh is one of the favorite cities of the people visiting the UK from all around the world. Known for its Art & literature, this place is filled with historical sites and monuments. Some of the popular destinations include Edinburgh Castle, the National Museum of Scotland, and the world-famous Arthur's Seat, a place filled with breathtaking views and also known as hiker's paradise. You're also likely to find some of the finest restaurants and unique boutiques in the city. So, explore the free museums or climb the top of Scott mountain, this place is worth every moment.

Glasgow

Glasgow is known for its rich heritage and welcoming people. The only city with an underground railway station in the country, the place is home to major businesses in the country making it a financial hub. Not to mention the sporty side of the city, hosted the 2014 commonwealth games which attracted sports lovers from all over the world and are now considered a major hub for football enthusiasts. Some of the common family-friendly destinations include RSPB Lochwinnoch Nature Reserve, lush green Barshaw Park, and the exotic wildlife of Clyde Muirshiel.

Aberdeen

Aberdeen is one of Scotland's most attractive towns, with famous granite structures that gleam in the sunlight and a golden coastline overlooking the North Sea. This ancient coastal city has fantastic shopping, a thriving nightlife, and hundreds of award-winning parks and gardens, making it a perfect city break destination. The neighboring Aberdeen Maritime Museum, a free and award-winning attraction that explains how important the North Sea oil and gas sectors have been to Aberdeen's success, may provide further information about the harbor's history.
